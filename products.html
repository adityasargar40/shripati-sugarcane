Our Production Excellence

At SHRIPATI SUGAR & POWER LTD, we take pride in our integrated approach to sugar production and power generation. Our state-of-the-art facilities and rigorous quality control processes ensure that we deliver products that meet the highest standards of quality and consistency.

From the careful selection of sugarcane to the final packaging of our sugar products, every step in our production process is monitored and optimized to maximize efficiency and minimize environmental impact.

Our Product Range

Sugar Products

Our sugar products are known for their purity, consistency, and quality. We produce a variety of sugar grades to meet different customer requirements.

  • Refined Sugar

    High-quality white crystalline sugar suitable for direct consumption and food processing industries.

  • Raw Sugar

    Unrefined sugar with a natural golden color, retaining more of the sugarcane's original flavor.

  • Industrial Sugar

    Specially processed sugar grades for pharmaceutical, beverage, and other industrial applications.

Power Generation

Our cogeneration power plant utilizes bagasse, a by-product of sugar production, to generate clean, renewable electricity.

  • Renewable Energy

    We generate electricity from bagasse, contributing to the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions and promoting sustainable energy practices.

  • Grid Supply

    Excess power is supplied to the local electricity grid, supporting the region's energy needs with clean power.

  • Self-Sufficiency

    Our power generation capabilities ensure that our factory operations are energy self-sufficient, reducing our reliance on external power sources.

By-Products

We maximize the value of sugarcane by utilizing various by-products of the sugar manufacturing process.

  • Molasses

    A thick, dark syrup that is a valuable input for the production of ethanol, yeast, and animal feed.

  • Press Mud

    An organic fertilizer rich in nutrients, beneficial for improving soil health and crop yields.

  • Bagasse

    The fibrous residue after crushing sugarcane, used as fuel for our power generation and as raw material for paper production.
